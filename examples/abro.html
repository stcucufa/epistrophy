ABRO example

O

The classic example from the synchronous programming language Esterel: O turns on when both A and B have been pressed, in any order; R resets the system. These two behaviours are implemented by the two top-level spawns. The first one is the most complex, as it consists of a sequence of two spawns as well (for buttons A and B) and a join, meaning that the first spawned fiber will progress only when both buttons have been clicked. O will turn on and stay on forever, thanks to the ramp with infinite duration. Clicking R on the other hand will end the second spawned fiber, and because the top-level join uses the First join delegate, it will end as soon as that fiber ends and cancel the other one. Regardless of whether it was waiting for A and/or B to be clicked or waiting forever, cancelling it moves to the ever block at the end which resets A, B and O to their original state.
